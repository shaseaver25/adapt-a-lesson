# Example Rubric: Persuasive Essay Analysis

## Assessment Description
Students will analyze a persuasive speech and identify rhetorical strategies used by the speaker.

## Learning Objectives
- Identify ethos, pathos, and logos in persuasive texts
- Evaluate the effectiveness of rhetorical strategies
- Support analysis with specific textual evidence

---

| Criteria | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Identification of Rhetorical Strategies** | Accurately identifies all three rhetorical appeals (ethos, pathos, logos) with precise examples from the text | Identifies all three rhetorical appeals with mostly accurate examples | Identifies two rhetorical appeals with some examples | Identifies one or fewer rhetorical appeals |
| **Analysis of Effectiveness** | Provides insightful analysis of how each strategy influences the audience, with specific evidence | Explains how strategies influence the audience with adequate evidence | Attempts to explain strategy effectiveness but lacks depth or evidence | Does not analyze effectiveness of strategies |
| **Use of Textual Evidence** | Integrates 3+ relevant, accurate quotes that directly support each claim | Uses 2-3 quotes that support most claims | Uses 1-2 quotes with limited connection to claims | Uses no quotes or irrelevant quotes |
| **Organization & Clarity** | Ideas flow logically with clear transitions; writing is polished and error-free | Ideas are organized with some transitions; few errors | Some organization present but transitions are weak; several errors | Lacks clear organization; many errors impede understanding |
