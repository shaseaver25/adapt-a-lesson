# Example: Authentic Assessment

## Assessment: The Water Cycle - Demonstrating Real Understanding

---

### Assessment Overview

**Method:** Community Problem-Based Project
**Duration:** 3 class periods
**Authentic Context:** Local water conservation

---

### The Challenge

Our community is experiencing drought conditions. The city council needs help educating residents about water conservation. Your task is to create an educational resource that explains the water cycle and how human actions affect our local water supply.

---

### Process Checkpoints (AI-Resistant)

**Checkpoint 1: Research & Observation**
- Observe and document a real example of evaporation (wet sidewalk, puddle, etc.)
- Take a photo with timestamp as evidence
- Write 3 sentences describing what you observed

**Checkpoint 2: Personal Connection**
- Interview a family member about water use in your home
- Record at least 3 questions and answers
- Teacher signature confirming authentic interview

**Checkpoint 3: Draft Review**
- Peer review session in class
- Explain your reasoning verbally to a partner
- Partner provides written feedback
- Revise based on feedback

---

### Final Product Options

Students choose ONE:

1. **Poster for City Hall** - Visual display explaining water cycle with conservation tips
2. **Children's Book** - Illustrated story teaching water cycle to K-2 students
3. **Video PSA** - 2-minute video for community social media
4. **Infographic** - Digital or hand-drawn data visualization

---

### Rubric Categories

| Criteria | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| Scientific Accuracy | All steps correct, detailed explanations | Steps correct, adequate explanations | Some errors, basic explanations | Multiple errors, incomplete |
| Real-World Connection | Strong local connection, specific examples | Clear connection, some examples | Weak connection | No connection made |
| Process Documentation | All checkpoints complete with detail | All checkpoints complete | Missing 1 checkpoint | Missing 2+ checkpoints |
| Communication | Clear, engaging, audience-appropriate | Clear and understandable | Somewhat unclear | Difficult to understand |

---

### Verification Features

✓ Timestamped photos required
✓ In-class peer review
✓ Verbal explanation to teacher
✓ Process folder with drafts
✓ Parent/family interaction documented

---

*This assessment was designed to be AI-resistant through process checkpoints, real-world artifacts, and in-person verification.*
